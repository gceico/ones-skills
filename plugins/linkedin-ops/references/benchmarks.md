# LinkedIn engagement benchmarks (white-label, generic)

Generic public-rule-of-thumb brackets — no audience-specific targets. Used by `analytics` to label
the user's numbers above/below median for their follower bracket. `engagement_rate = engagements / impressions * 100`.

## Engagement rate by follower count

| Followers | Poor | Average | Good | Excellent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0–1,000 | <2% | 2–4% | 4–8% | >8% |
| 1,000–5,000 | <1.5% | 1.5–3% | 3–6% | >6% |
| 5,000–10,000 | <1% | 1–2.5% | 2.5–5% | >5% |
| 10,000–50,000 | <0.8% | 0.8–2% | 2–4% | >4% |
| 50,000+ | <0.5% | 0.5–1.5% | 1.5–3% | >3% |

Smaller accounts run higher rates (tighter, more engaged audiences). Compare a post to its own
account's bracket, not to a megacreator.

## Format multipliers (rough, relative to a plain text post = 1.0)

| Format | Typical reach/engagement multiplier |
|---|---|
| Text (with strong hook) | 1.0 |
| Carousel / document | 1.3–1.8× (high dwell time) |
| Poll | 1.2–1.5× reach, lower-quality engagement |
| Image / infographic | 1.1–1.3× |
| Native video | 0.8–1.2× (variable) |
| External link in body | 0.5–0.7× (suppressed; prefer link in comments) |

Treat these as priors to sanity-check the user's own data, not as ground truth — always defer to what
their `analytics.csv` actually shows for their account.

## Cadence

3–5 posts/week is the common consistency band for growth. Below ~1/week, momentum and reach decay.
Report the user's actual posts/week and gaps; don't prescribe a number beyond flagging long silences.
