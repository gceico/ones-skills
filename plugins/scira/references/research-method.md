# Research method — discourse map + trends (ops, not creative)

The engine for `research`. Goal: surface what's being discussed and trending in a topic/niche,
with sources — NOT to generate post ideas in anyone's voice. Widen, then organize. Stop at the brief.

Order matters: map before ranking. Tag every material claim inline with one of:
`[backed by source]` (cite the last30days result / URL), `[reasonable inference]`, `[speculative]`.
Keep facts, inferences, and guesses visibly separate.

## Stage 1 — Read the disruption

What materially moved this topic in the window? New model/tool releases, a debate everyone's reacting
to, a benchmark, a regulation milestone, a news event being news-jacked. Pull from last30days + web.

## Stage 2 — Discourse canvas

Bucket the live sub-topics into three columns:

- **Saturated** — high supply, everyone's posting it. (Crowded.)
- **Emerging** — rising signal, a few voices on it, not yet crowded. (Best timing.)
- **White-space** — real curiosity/questions in the discourse (engagement, recurring comments) with
  thin or no good coverage. (Open lane.)

For each, note where the signal comes from (which platform, what engagement) and who covers it.

## Stage 3 — Key trends & shifts

Rank the trends by signal strength (engagement + cross-platform presence). For each: what it is, who's
driving it, where it's heading, and the strongest source. This is the core of the brief.

## Stage 4 — Open / under-answered questions

Questions people keep asking that nobody answers well — the "does anyone know…", the recurring comment
threads. These are the white-space markers worth flagging.

## Stage 5 — Cross-domain angles

Frameworks or findings from adjacent fields (research, other industries, ops, design) being applied —
or not yet applied — to this topic. Anchor each to a real source.

## Stage 6 — Weak signals

2–3 early signals (a tool gaining traction, a regulation timeline, a shift in what's being asked) that
hint where the topic is heading before it's obvious.

## Output shape

`reports/research-<slug>-YYYY-MM-DD.md`:

1. **Takeaway** — one paragraph: what's the state of this topic right now.
2. **Discourse map** — the three columns.
3. **Trends** — ranked, each with source + direction.
4. **Open questions** — the under-answered list.
5. **Weak signals** — 2–3.
6. **Sources** — flat list of links used (inline links are also fine throughout).

No "seeds in my voice", no pillars, no calendar. This brief is an input the user reads — they decide
what to do with it.
